In 1913, the magazine was purchased by the banking firm
of White Weld & Co., which organized the McCall Corporation
under the direction of president Edward Alfred Simmons. In
1917, the price was raised to 10 cents per issue.[5] In 1922,
Harry Payne Burton became editor, and for the first time such
well-known fiction writers as Kathleen Norris, Harold Bell Wright,
Zane Grey and Booth Tarkington had stories published in McCall's.
